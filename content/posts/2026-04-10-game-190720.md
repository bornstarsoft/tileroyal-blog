In a recent interview, Jon Gibson, head of transformation at Keywords Studios, revealed a startling truth about AI tools in game development: out of over 500 tested, only about six have proven genuinely useful. This stark reality highlights the chaotic phase AI currently finds itself in, where many tools are created without a clear understanding of the specific problems they aim to solve.

Gibson emphasizes that while 90% of developers are using AI, a significant 52% disapprove of it, indicating a disconnect between usage and satisfaction. The challenge lies in moving from flashy AI demos to practical applications that enhance production quality and team collaboration. As the industry grapples with these issues, the need for governance and clarity in AI usage becomes increasingly critical.

As we look to the future, the question remains: how can the gaming industry harness AI effectively to not only improve development processes but also ensure that it complements rather than complicates team dynamics?
